# I. æ ¸å¿ƒæ¦‚å¿µ (Concepts & Definitions)
*Defining what Red Tape is (and is not).*

* **[Bozeman, B. (1993). A theory of government "red tape".](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=A+theory+of+government+red+tape+Bozeman)**
    * *Core Definition:* "Rules, regulations, and procedures that remain in force and entail a compliance burden, but do not advance the legitimate object of the rule."
* **[Bozeman, B. (2000). Bureaucracy and Red Tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Bureaucracy+and+Red+Tape+Bozeman)**
    * *Distinction:* **Formalization** (neutral) vs. **Red Tape** (negative pathology).
* **[Pandey, S. K., & Scott, P. G. (2002). Red tape: A review and assessment of concepts and measures.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Red+tape+review+Pandey+Scott)**
    * *Concept:* Consolidates the distinction between subjective (perceived) and objective red tape.
* **[Van Loon, N. M., et al. (2016). The bright and dark side of working in the public sector.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=The+bright+and+dark+side+of+working+in+the+public+sector)**
    * *Concept:* Discusses red tape as a key "job demand" in the Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) model.
* **[DeHart-Davis, L. (2009). Green tape: A theory of effective rules.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Green+tape+DeHart-Davis)**
    * *Counter-Concept:* **Green Tape** (effective rules) has five attributes: written, valid, optimal control, consistent, and logically understandable.

# II. æµ‹é‡å·¥å…· (Measurement Scales)
*Key scales used in quantitative research.*

* **[Rainey, H. G., et al. (1995). Public and private managers' perceptions of red tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Public+and+private+managers+perceptions+of+red+tape)**
    * *Scale:* **General Red Tape Scale**. (e.g., "The rules and regulations in this organization are often effectively meaningless.")
* **[Bozeman, B., & Feeney, M. K. (2011). Rules and red tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Rules+and+red+tape+Bozeman+Feeney)**
    * *Scale:* **Organizational Red Tape Scale**. Focuses on the burden on the organization's core mission.
* **[Welch, E. W., & Pandey, S. K. (2007). E-government and bureaucracy.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=E-government+and+bureaucracy+Welch)**
    * *Scale:* **Electronic Red Tape**. Measuring burdens arising from IT systems.
* **[Jacobsen, C. B., & Jakobsen, M. L. (2018). Perceived organizational red tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Perceived+organizational+red+tape+Jacobsen)**
    * *Validation:* Provides rigorous psychometric validation of red tape scales.

# III. æ³•ç†ç½‘ç»œï¼šå‰å›  (Antecedents)
*What causes Red Tape? (Based on the Nomological Network)*

### 1. å¤–éƒ¨ç¯å¢ƒ (Environmental/Political)
* **[Brewer, G. A., et al. (2012). External control and red tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=External+control+and+red+tape+Brewer)**
    * *Factor:* **Political Control**. High external accountability pressure leads to more internal rules (red tape).
* **[Feeney, M. K. (2012). Organizational attributes and red tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Organizational+attributes+and+red+tape+Feeney)**
    * *Factor:* **Sector Differences**. Comparisons between public, non-profit, and private sectors.
* **[Pandey, S. K. (2010). Cutback management and the paradox of red tape.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Cutback+management+and+the+paradox+of+red+tape)**
    * *Factor:* **Fiscal Constraints**. Budget cuts often *increase* red tape due to increased monitoring.

### 2. ç»„ç»‡ç‰¹å¾ (Organizational Structural)
* **[DeHart-Davis, L., & Pandey, S. K. (2005). Red tape and public employees.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Red+tape+and+public+employees+DeHart-Davis)**
    * *Factor:* **Centralization & Hierarchy**. Highly centralized structures are positively correlated with red tape perceptions.
* **[Walker, R. M., & Brewer, G. A. (2009). Can management strategy minimize the impact of red tape?](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Can+management+strategy+minimize+the+impact+of+red+tape)**
    * *Factor:* **Management Strategy**. Prospector strategies may face different red tape hurdles than defenders.
* **[Moon, M. J., & Bretschneider, S. (2002). Does the perception of red tape differ?](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Does+the+perception+of+red+tape+differ+Moon)**
    * *Factor:* **IT Investment**. The relationship between IT and red tape is complex (sometimes reducing, sometimes creating "virtual red tape").

### 3. ä¸ªä½“ç‰¹å¾ (Individual/Demographic)
* **[Scott, P. G., & Pandey, S. K. (2005). Red tape and public service motivation.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Red+tape+and+public+service+motivation+Scott)**
    * *Factor:* **Managerial Experience**. Longer tenure can sometimes lead to "rule habitation" (getting used to red tape).
* **[Stazyk, E. C., et al. (2011). Organizational structure and social desirability.](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Organizational+structure+and+social+desirability)**
    * *Factor:* **Perception Bias**. How individual personality traits affect the reporting of red tape.
